### Comparative Negligence Models by State

#### **Contributory Negligence (COMPLETE BAR) - 4 Remaining States + DC** ⚠️
These are the LAST states using this harsh doctrine where ANY plaintiff fault (even 1%) bars ALL recovery:
1. **Alabama** (AL)
2. **Maryland** (MD)
3. **North Carolina** (NC)
4. **Virginia** (VA)
5. **District of Columbia** (DC) - not yet seeded

**Critical Warning:** These states require EXTREMELY careful pleading and evidence to avoid complete bar.

#### **Pure Comparative Negligence - 12 States** 📊
Plaintiff can recover even if 99% at fault (damages reduced by fault %):
1. **Alaska** (AK)
2. **California** (CA)
3. **Florida** (FL) - switched from pure to modified in 2023, but still treated as pure for pre-2023 cases
4. **Kentucky** (KY)
5. **Louisiana** (LA) - civil law system
6. **Mississippi** (MS)
7. **Missouri** (MO)
8. **New York** (NY) - not yet seeded
9. **Rhode Island** (RI)
10. **South Dakota** (SD) - corrected to modified in latest files
11. **Washington** (WA)
12. **West Virginia** (WV)

#### **Modified Comparative - 51% Bar (Majority) - 30+ States** 🏛️
Plaintiff can recover if fault is 50% or less (barred at 51%+):
- Most common model nationwide
- Includes: CO, CT, DE, HI, IL, IN, IA, MA, MI, MN, MT, NV, NH, NJ, ND, OK, OR, SC, SD, TX, VT, WI, WY

#### **Modified Comparative - 50% Bar (Stricter) - 5 States** ⚖️
Plaintiff must be LESS than 50% at fault (barred at exactly 50%):
1. **Arizona** (AZ)
2. **Arkansas** (AR)
3. **Georgia** (GA) - strict "at or below" language
4. **Idaho** (ID) - strict interpretation
5. **Kansas** (KS) - strict interpretation

### Special Doctrines by State

#### **Safe Place Statute - Wisconsin** 🏢
- **Wis. Stat. § 101.11**
- HIGHER standard than ordinary negligence
- Strict liability for structural defects
- Applies to places of employment and public buildings

#### **Mode-of-Operation Rule - New Jersey** 🛒
- Relaxed notice requirement
- Self-service establishments
- Focus on business method rather than specific hazard notice

#### **Transitory Foreign Substance - Florida** 💧
- **F.S. § 768.0755**
- Higher burden for slippery floors
- Must prove: (1) actual knowledge, OR (2) constructive knowledge through regular occurrence

#### **Superior Knowledge Rule - Georgia** 🎯
- **O.C.G.A. § 51-3-1**
- Unique to Georgia
- Plaintiff must show owner had superior knowledge of hazard
- Robinson v. Kroger two-prong test

#### **Hills and Ridges Doctrine - Pennsylvania** ❄️
- Winter weather protection
- No duty during ongoing storms
- Limited liability for natural accumulation

#### **Open & Obvious Eliminates Duty - Ohio** 👁️
- Unique approach
- Open & obvious hazards eliminate duty entirely
- Stricter than other states (most just reduce damages)
